# PanelOptimizer

## Project Vision

---

### The Problem

Large artistic panels often exceed the printable volume of desktop FDM printers.

They must therefore be divided into several pieces.

Current CAD software focuses on producing printable parts.

Very little attention is paid to what happens after printing.

However, the real objective is not printing.

The real objective is the finished artwork.

---

### The Missing Step

The current workflow is:

Design

↓

Split

↓

Print

↓

Glue

↓

Fill

↓

Sand

↓

Paint

↓

Finished artwork

Today's software optimizes only the split.

The remaining operations are entirely manual.

This is precisely where the visible joint appears.

---

### Our Vision

PanelOptimizer changes the optimization target.

It does not optimise the split.

It optimises the final repaired surface.

The split is only a consequence.

---

### Invisible Joint Philosophy

A successful split is not one that produces printable pieces.

A successful split is one that completely disappears after finishing.

The finished artwork should not reveal where it was divided.

Not visually.

Not by touch.

Not under grazing light.

---

### Engineering Philosophy

The software combines several disciplines.

Geometry

Topology

Additive Manufacturing

Mechanical Assembly

Surface Repair

Finishing

Instead of treating them independently, PanelOptimizer considers them simultaneously.

---

### Long-Term Goal

Become the reference software for preparing large artistic pieces for invisible assembly after FDM printing.

---

### Guiding Principle

The software does not design cuts.

The software designs invisible repairs.

Everything else is a consequence.
